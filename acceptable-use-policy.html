1 · Purpose
POL-AUP-002 · Section 1

Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines the standards for the appropriate and secure use of NovaTech GmbH's information assets, technology systems, cloud services, and client data. It is a subordinate document of the Information Security Policy (POL-ISP-001) and implements ISO/IEC 27001:2022 controls A.5.10 and A.6.7.

Given that NovaTech operates as a data processor under GDPR Article 28, handling personal behavioral data of EU residents on behalf of client controllers, this policy places particular emphasis on the acceptable use of client data and the restrictions that apply to all staff regardless of role or seniority.

2 · Scope
POL-AUP-002 · Section 2

Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who access NovaTech systems or data, including:

  • All full-time and part-time employees of NovaTech GmbH
  • All contractors, consultants, and freelancers engaged by NovaTech
  • Third-party suppliers with access to NovaTech systems or client data
  • Any individual using a personally-owned device (BYOD) to access NovaTech resources

This policy covers all NovaTech information assets including the AI-powered analytics platform, AWS infrastructure, SaaS tools, client data, source code, internal communications, and all devices used for work purposes.

3 · General Use of Systems
POL-AUP-002 · Section 3

General use of information systems

NovaTech provides access to information systems and tools for the purpose of conducting legitimate business activities. The following rules apply to all system usage:

✓ Permitted

  • Accessing systems and data required to perform assigned job responsibilities
  • Using approved communication tools (Whereby, Brevo, company email) for business purposes
  • Storing work files on approved systems (AWS S3, approved cloud tools)
  • Using the analytics platform to serve client needs per controller instructions
  • Accessing NovaTech systems remotely via approved and secured devices
  • Using AI-assisted tools for productivity when client data is not involved

✗ Prohibited

  • Accessing systems, data, or AWS resources beyond what is required for your role
  • Using NovaTech systems for personal business, commercial activities, or entertainment
  • Storing client personal data on personal devices, USB drives, or unapproved cloud services
  • Sharing login credentials, AWS access keys, or API keys with any other person
  • Circumventing security controls, MFA requirements, or access restrictions
  • Installing unapproved software on devices used to access NovaTech systems
4 · Client Data Handling
POL-AUP-002 · Section 4

Acceptable use of client personal data

Client behavioral data processed through NovaTech's analytics platform is classified as Restricted under the Information Security Policy. As a data processor under GDPR Art. 28, NovaTech staff must observe the following rules without exception:

  • Client personal data must only be processed for the specific purposes defined in the Data Processing Agreement with each client controller. Any other use is strictly prohibited.
  • Client data must never be used to train, test, or improve NovaTech's AI models without explicit written authorization from the data controller.
  • Client data must never be copied, downloaded, or transferred to personal devices, personal email accounts, or any service not listed in the approved tools list.
  • If you suspect client data has been accessed, copied, or disclosed without authorization, report it to the GRC Specialist immediately. Do not attempt to investigate independently.
  • Access to client data is granted on a need-to-know basis only. If your role does not require access to a specific client dataset, do not attempt to access it.
  • When using client data in test or development environments, pseudonymized or synthetic data must be used. Real client personal data must never be used in non-production environments.
5 · AWS & Cloud Services
POL-AUP-002 · Section 5

Acceptable use of AWS and cloud services

NovaTech's infrastructure runs exclusively on AWS eu-central-1 (Frankfurt). All access to the AWS console, CLI, and APIs must comply with the following rules:

  • The AWS root account must never be used for day-to-day operations. All access must be through individual IAM users with the minimum permissions required for the role.
  • MFA must be enabled on all AWS IAM accounts. Accessing the AWS console without MFA is a policy violation.
  • AWS access keys must never be hardcoded in source code, committed to Git repositories, or shared via email or messaging tools. All secrets must be stored in AWS Secrets Manager.
  • No NovaTech data or workloads may be moved to AWS regions outside eu-central-1 without written approval from the CEO, to preserve GDPR data residency requirements.
  • S3 buckets must never be configured with public access. All buckets containing client data must have S3 Block Public Access enabled and SSE-KMS encryption active.
  • New AWS services or configurations that affect security group rules, IAM policies, or data storage must be reviewed by the Tech Lead before deployment to production.

7 · BYOD Policy
POL-AUP-002 · Section 7

Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

NovaTech permits the use of personally-owned devices for work purposes subject to the following mandatory requirements. Non-compliance with BYOD requirements may result in access being revoked.

Required on all BYOD devices

  • Full-disk encryption enabled (FileVault on macOS, BitLocker on Windows)
  • Screen lock with PIN, password, or biometric, activating after maximum 5 minutes of inactivity
  • Operating system and applications kept up to date with latest security patches
  • MFA enabled on all accounts used to access NovaTech systems
  • Antivirus or endpoint protection software installed and active
  • Device reported to GRC Specialist immediately if lost or stolen

Prohibited on BYOD devices

  • Storing client personal data locally on the device outside of approved secure applications
  • Sharing the device with family members or other individuals while logged into NovaTech systems
  • Using public Wi-Fi to access the AWS console or client data without a VPN
  • Jailbreaking or rooting the device
  • Disabling security controls required by this policy
  • Continuing to use a device after it has been reported lost or stolen

Upon termination of employment or contract, the individual must allow NovaTech to verify that all company data has been removed from the personal device. NovaTech will not perform a remote wipe of personal devices without the individual's explicit consent, except where required by law.

8 · Email & Communications
POL-AUP-002 · Section 8

Email and communications

All business communications must use NovaTech-approved channels. The following rules apply to email and messaging:

  • Client personal data must never be transmitted via email, Whereby chat, or any messaging tool unless encrypted and explicitly authorized. Use secure file transfer via AWS S3 pre-signed URLs where required.
  • Personal email accounts must never be used for NovaTech business communications or to send, receive, or store any NovaTech or client data.
  • Be vigilant about phishing emails. Do not click links or open attachments from unknown senders. Report suspicious emails to the GRC Specialist immediately.
  • Mass email communications to clients or prospects must only be sent via Brevo using approved templates. Unsolicited marketing to EU residents must comply with GDPR consent requirements and TTDSG.
  • Email domains used for business communication must have SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured. Do not use unapproved domains for business email.
9 · Internet Use
POL-AUP-002 · Section 9

Internet and web use

Internet access is provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is tolerated provided it does not interfere with work or compromise NovaTech's security posture. The following activities are prohibited:

  • Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content of any kind
  • Visiting websites known to distribute malware or engaging in activities that could introduce malware to NovaTech systems
  • Downloading software or browser extensions onto work devices without approval from the Tech Lead
  • Using NovaTech internet access or systems for cryptocurrency mining, gaming, or streaming for personal entertainment during work hours
  • Accessing social media for personal use in a manner that interferes with productivity or that could create reputational risk for NovaTech
10 · Violations
POL-AUP-002 · Section 10

Policy violations

Violations of this policy will be handled in accordance with the disciplinary procedures defined in the Information Security Policy (POL-ISP-001) and NovaTech's HR policies. Violations involving client personal data may additionally trigger GDPR breach notification obligations to the affected data controller.

  • Intentional misuse of client data or deliberate circumvention of security controls may result in immediate termination and potential legal action.
  • Repeated or negligent violations of BYOD, cloud, or communication rules will result in formal disciplinary action and potential revocation of system access.
  • First-time minor violations will typically result in a written warning and mandatory retraining.
11 · Review & Maintenance
POL-AUP-002 · Section 11

Policy review

This policy is reviewed annually or when triggered by a significant change in technology, regulatory requirements, or a security incident. All changes must be approved by the CEO before taking effect.
