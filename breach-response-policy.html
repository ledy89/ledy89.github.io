1 · Purpose
POL-DBR-003 · Section 1

Purpose

This Data Breach Response Policy establishes NovaTech GmbH's procedure for identifying, containing, assessing, notifying, and documenting personal data breaches. It implements ISO/IEC 27001:2022 controls A.5.24 and A.5.26 and fulfills NovaTech's obligations under GDPR Articles 33 and 34.

As a data processor under GDPR Article 28, NovaTech processes personal behavioral data of EU residents on behalf of client data controllers. This role creates a specific and time-sensitive notification obligation: upon becoming aware of a personal data breach, NovaTech must notify the affected data controller without undue delay so the controller can fulfill its own 72-hour notification obligation to the supervisory authority. NovaTech's internal escalation target is therefore 24 hours from detection, not 72 hours.

2 · Scope
POL-DBR-003 · Section 2

Scope and definitions

This policy applies to all personal data processed by NovaTech GmbH in its role as a data processor, including client behavioral data, user registration data, and any other personal data stored in AWS eu-central-1 infrastructure or processed through NovaTech's analytics platform.

Definition of a personal data breach

Under GDPR Art. 4(12), a personal data breach is a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed. This includes:

  • Unauthorized access to client behavioral data in AWS S3, RDS, or the analytics platform
  • Accidental deletion or corruption of client personal data without backup recovery
  • Ransomware or malware encrypting systems containing client personal data
  • Misconfigured AWS S3 bucket exposing client data to public access
  • Unauthorized disclosure of client data to a third party not authorized in the DPA
  • Loss or theft of a BYOD device containing unencrypted client personal data
  • AWS IAM credential compromise leading to unauthorized access to client data
3 · Breach Severity Classification
POL-DBR-003 · Section 3

Breach severity levels

All suspected breaches must be classified immediately upon detection. Severity determines the speed and scope of the response. When in doubt, always classify at the higher severity level.

Critical

Immediate response required

Large-scale unauthorized access to client personal data. Ransomware on production systems. Public S3 bucket exposure of client data. AWS root account compromise. Estimated data subjects affected: 1,000+.

High

Response within 2 hours

Unauthorized access to a subset of client data. IAM credential compromise with evidence of data access. Loss of encrypted BYOD device with client data. Estimated data subjects affected: 100–999.

Low

Response within 8 hours

Accidental internal disclosure of client data to unauthorized NovaTech employee. Loss of encrypted device with no evidence of data access. Small-scale unintentional data exposure. Estimated data subjects affected: fewer than 100.

5 · Notification Decision
POL-DBR-003 · Section 5

Does this breach require notification?

Not all security incidents are notifiable breaches. Use the decision framework below to determine notification obligations. When in doubt, notify. Failing to notify a controller of a qualifying breach is a GDPR violation.

Q1: Has personal data been accessed, disclosed, altered, destroyed, or lost without authorization?
YES → Continue to Q2

This is a personal data breach. Proceed with the response procedure and assess notification obligations.

NO → Security incident only

Document as a security incident. No GDPR notification required. Monitor and apply lessons learned.

Q2: Is the breach likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons?
YES → Notify the controller

Notify the affected data controller without undue delay and within 24 hours of NovaTech becoming aware. The controller will then assess supervisory authority notification.

NO → Document and monitor

No notification required but the breach must be documented in the incident register per GDPR Art. 33(5). Review classification if new information emerges.

6 · Response Timeline
POL-DBR-003 · Section 6

Response procedure and timeline

The following timeline applies from the moment a breach is detected or suspected. All times are maximum targets. Critical incidents should move faster than the stated deadlines.

T+0
Detect

Detection and initial report

Any employee, system alert, or third party reports a suspected breach. The person detecting the incident must notify the GRC Specialist immediately via direct message or phone. Do not attempt to investigate independently.

  • Note the time of detection and all known facts
  • Do not delete, modify, or power off affected systems
  • If AWS GuardDuty or CloudWatch alerts are the source, capture the full alert details
T+1h
Assess

Initial assessment and severity classification

GRC Specialist and Tech Lead assess the incident. Classify severity (Critical, High, Low). Confirm whether personal data is involved and estimate the scope.

  • Review AWS CloudTrail logs for unauthorized API calls
  • Check S3 access logs for unauthorized data access or downloads
  • Identify which client data may be affected and estimate number of data subjects
  • Open the incident register and assign an incident ID
T+2h
Contain

Containment

Tech Lead executes technical containment to stop the breach from spreading. GRC Specialist documents all actions taken.

  • Revoke compromised AWS IAM credentials and rotate affected access keys immediately
  • Enable S3 Block Public Access if a misconfigured bucket is the source
  • Isolate affected EC2 instances by modifying security group rules
  • Preserve all AWS CloudTrail logs and GuardDuty findings as evidence — do not delete
  • If a BYOD device is lost or stolen, initiate remote data verification procedure
T+24h
Notify

Controller notification

GRC Specialist drafts and sends breach notification to all affected data controllers. This is NovaTech's primary notification obligation as a data processor under GDPR Art. 28(3)(f). The controller then has 72 hours from their own awareness to notify the supervisory authority.

  • Send notification to the designated contact in the client's DPA
  • Include all information known at this stage — incomplete notifications are acceptable under GDPR Art. 33(4)
  • CEO must approve the notification before sending
  • Document the exact time of notification in the incident register
T+72h
Update

Follow-up notification and full assessment

Send updated notification to controllers with full assessment findings if not all information was available at T+24h. Support controllers in responding to any supervisory authority inquiries.

  • Provide confirmed number of affected data subjects and categories of data
  • Confirm containment measures taken and their effectiveness
  • Describe measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate future risk
T+30d
Review

Post-incident review and remediation

GRC Specialist leads a post-incident review within 30 days. Identify root cause, control failures, and lessons learned. Update the risk register and implement remediation actions.

  • Document root cause analysis in the incident register
  • Update the risk register to reflect any new or changed risks identified
  • Implement and track remediation actions with defined owners and deadlines
  • Report findings to CEO and update relevant policies if required
  • Close the incident in the register once all remediation actions are verified

Use the following template for the initial notification to affected data controllers. Customize with the specific facts of the incident. Incomplete information should be noted as "under investigation" — do not delay notification to gather all facts.

8 · Evidence Preservation
POL-DBR-003 · Section 8

Evidence preservation on AWS

Evidence must be preserved from the moment a breach is suspected. Deleting or modifying evidence can constitute a GDPR violation and may create legal liability. The Tech Lead is responsible for evidence preservation on AWS infrastructure.

  • Do not disable, delete, or modify AWS CloudTrail logs under any circumstances during an active incident. CloudTrail is the primary evidence source for all AWS activity.
  • Do not power off or terminate affected EC2 instances before capturing a snapshot. Memory forensics may be required for certain incident types.
  • Export and archive AWS GuardDuty findings, CloudWatch alarms, and VPC Flow Logs related to the incident to a dedicated S3 bucket with Object Lock enabled.
  • Capture S3 server access logs for all buckets potentially involved in the breach.
  • Document the chain of custody for all evidence: who collected it, when, from which AWS service, and where it is stored.
  • Set the evidence S3 bucket retention policy to a minimum of 3 years or as required by applicable law, whichever is longer.
9 · Incident Register
POL-DBR-003 · Section 9

Incident register requirements

GDPR Art. 33(5) requires data processors to document all personal data breaches, regardless of whether notification was required. The incident register must capture the following information for every incident:

  • Incident ID, date and time of detection, and date and time of containment
  • Description of the breach: nature, systems affected, AWS services involved
  • Categories and approximate number of personal data records affected
  • Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
  • Severity classification and rationale
  • Containment actions taken and by whom
  • Date and time of controller notification and name of controller contact notified
  • Root cause analysis findings and remediation actions with owners and deadlines
  • Final incident closure date and sign-off by GRC Specialist and CEO

The incident register must be retained for a minimum of 3 years and made available to data controllers upon request as part of NovaTech's accountability obligations under GDPR Art. 5(2).

10 · Review & Maintenance
POL-DBR-003 · Section 10

Policy review

This policy is reviewed annually or immediately following a significant security incident or change in regulatory requirements. The response procedure must be tested at least once per year through a tabletop exercise simulating a breach scenario.
