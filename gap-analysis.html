01 · Executive Summary

Assessment overview

NovaTech GmbH is a simulated Berlin-based technology startup of 30 employees that develops an AI-powered customer analytics platform. The platform ingests behavioral, transactional, and engagement data from client websites and applications, applies machine learning models to generate churn predictions, customer segmentation, and actionable insights, and delivers them via a real-time dashboard. NovaTech acts as a data processor (GDPR Art. 28) for its clients — European mid-market companies — who remain the data controllers for their end users' personal data.

The company hosts its entire infrastructure on AWS eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) and relies exclusively on EU-based third-party suppliers for communications, document signing, scheduling, analytics, and email. Despite this privacy-conscious supplier strategy, the assessment reveals an overall maturity of 1.6 out of 4.0. Critical gaps are concentrated in AWS cloud configuration, access control, incident response, and ISMS documentation. These gaps create compounded regulatory exposure under GDPR, the EU AI Act (Annex III high-risk classification for AI-based profiling), and NIS2.

05 · Third-Party Assessment

Supplier and cloud security posture

NovaTech GmbH has deliberately selected EU-based suppliers for all operational functions, significantly reducing cross-border data transfer risk and GDPR compliance complexity. AWS operates as the sole cloud infrastructure provider under the shared responsibility model. NovaTech is fully responsible for all configurations within its AWS environment.

Note: as a data processor itself, NovaTech must also maintain up-to-date Data Processing Agreements with each of its own clients (the data controllers), in addition to the DPAs it holds with the suppliers below.

06 · Implementation Roadmap

The three-phase roadmap integrates AWS-native security controls throughout to accelerate implementation. AWS services such as GuardDuty, CloudTrail, Macie, Inspector, WAF, Config, and Security Hub directly address multiple ISO 27001 Annex A controls. Target: ISO 27001 certification readiness in 12 months.

07 · Conclusions

Key findings

NovaTech GmbH is a simulated Berlin-based technology startup of 30 employees that develops an AI-powered customer analytics platform. The company processes personal data of EU residents and hosts its infrastructure on AWS. The gap analysis reveals an overall maturity of 1.6/4.0, with critical exposures in AWS cloud configuration, access control, incident response, and ISMS documentation.

NovaTech's role as a GDPR data processor introduces specific obligations that go beyond standard controller compliance: it must maintain a RoPA of its own processing activities, notify controllers promptly upon detecting a breach, engage only sub-processors authorized by controllers, and demonstrate compliance on demand. None of these obligations are currently supported by documented procedures, which represents the highest regulatory risk in the assessment.

The deliberate selection of EU-based suppliers (Scrive, Whereby, Matomo, Piwik PRO, Brevo, Cal.com EU) is a well-reasoned GRC decision that eliminates cross-border transfer complexity and aligns with German DPA guidance. This should be formally documented as a risk treatment choice in the ISMS. Combined with AWS eu-central-1 as the exclusive infrastructure region, NovaTech has a strong data residency foundation to build upon. With the three-phase roadmap and AWS-native controls, ISO 27001 certification readiness is achievable within 12 months.
