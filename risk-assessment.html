01 · Executive Summary

Overview

NovaTech GmbH is a simulated Berlin-based technology startup of 30 employees that develops an AI-powered customer analytics platform. The company processes personal data of EU residents and hosts its infrastructure on AWS and GCP. This risk assessment was conducted to identify information security risks, evaluate their potential impact on the business, and recommend prioritized mitigation actions aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and NIST CSF.

The assessment identified 12 risks across five domains: data protection, access control, third-party management, AI governance, and infrastructure security. Three risks were rated Critical and four High, requiring immediate remediation action.

02 · Scope & Methodology

What was assessed

The assessment covers NovaTech GmbH's core information assets, business processes, and technology infrastructure. The following domains were included in scope:

  • Customer personal data processing systems (GDPR Art. 5 & 25 obligations)
  • AI-based analytics platform (EU AI Act Annex III high-risk classification)
  • Cloud infrastructure hosted on AWS and GCP
  • Third-party vendor relationships and data processing agreements
  • Internal access control and identity management systems
  • Incident response and business continuity capabilities

06 · Treatment Plan

Priority remediation actions

The following treatment actions address the three Critical and four High risks. Each action includes the responsible owner, target deadline, and success metric.

R01 · Unauthorized access to customer personal data

Implement role-based access control (RBAC), enforce encryption at rest and in transit (AES-256 / TLS 1.3), and enable full access logging with alerting on anomalous behavior.

Owner: IT Lead + DPO Deadline: 7 days ISO A.5.15 · A.8.3 NIST: Protect
Critical

R02 · AI model processing personal data without legal basis

Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for all AI features. Document lawful basis per GDPR Art. 6 for each processing activity. Perform EU AI Act Annex III conformity assessment and register system in the EU AI database if applicable.

Owner: DPO + CTO Deadline: 14 days ISO A.5.31 · A.5.34 GDPR Art. 35
Critical

R03 · Absence of data breach response procedure

Develop and test an Incident Response Plan. Define escalation paths, evidence preservation procedures, and a 72-hour GDPR notification workflow to supervisory authorities (BSI / DPA Berlin).

Owner: CISO Deadline: 14 days ISO A.5.26 · A.5.28 GDPR Art. 33
Critical

R04 · Third-party vendors without valid DPA

Audit all vendor contracts and identify processors lacking a signed DPA. Enforce DPA signing within 30 days. Document cross-border transfer mechanisms (SCCs, DPF, BCR) for any non-EU processors.

Owner: Legal / DPO Deadline: 30 days ISO A.5.19 · A.5.20 GDPR Art. 28
High

R06 · Absence of MFA on critical systems

Enforce multi-factor authentication on all administrator accounts, cloud consoles (AWS, GCP), and remote access systems. Use phishing-resistant methods (FIDO2 / hardware keys) for privileged accounts.

Owner: IT Lead Deadline: 14 days ISO A.5.17 · A.8.2 NIST: Protect
High
07 · Conclusions & Next Steps

Key findings and recommendations

NovaTech GmbH presents a risk profile typical of early-stage AI startups operating in regulated European markets: strong product velocity but significant gaps in governance, data protection controls, and documented security processes. The convergence of GDPR, EU AI Act, and ISO 27001 obligations creates compounded regulatory exposure that must be addressed before any external audit or enterprise sales process.

The three Critical risks (R01, R02, R03) share a common root cause: the absence of a formal ISMS with documented ownership, defined controls, and tested procedures. Implementing these foundational elements will simultaneously reduce multiple risks across the register.

Recommended next steps are to establish a GRC steering committee with defined roles (CISO, DPO, IT Lead), complete the treatment actions for all Critical and High risks within 30 days, and initiate an ISO 27001 implementation project with a target certification timeline of 12-18 months.
